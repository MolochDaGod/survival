# The Grudges Compendium

---

## A Note on the Timeline

All dates are marked relative to two systems: **Old Earth Years (OEY)** — the calendar we still use — and **Post-Contact (PC)** , which counts from the year of The Way's arrival. The current year is **2398 OEY** (343 PC).

---

# Part I: The History

## Chapter 1: Before the Fall — The Dying World (2000–2055)

By the mid-21st century, Earth was in terminal decline. Climate systems had collapsed. The sixth mass extinction was well underway. Wars were fought over fresh water, arable land, and breathable air. Humanity, brilliant and self-destructive, had painted itself into a corner.

But we didn't know how deep the hole really was.

The scientists knew. The paleoclimatologists, the geophysicists, the astronomers — they saw the data. A cascade of feedback loops had been triggered that no intervention could stop. The planet's core was destabilizing. The magnetosphere was weakening. The estimate was 300 years, maybe 400, before Earth became uninhabitable.

They kept it quiet. Or tried to.

It didn't matter in the end. Because in 2055, they came.

---

## Chapter 2: The Arrival (2055 — Year 0 PC)

They emerged from a warp tear two hundred kilometers above the Pacific Ocean. No invasion force. No ultimatum delivered through firepower. Just a single vessel, smooth and silent, hanging in geostationary orbit for seventy-two hours while the world panicked.

When they finally descended, they did so in every major city simultaneously. Tens of thousands of them — each one indistinguishable from baseline humanity. Same biology. Same anatomy. Same cellular structure. They were us, separated by tens of thousands of years of divergent evolution on some distant Earth-like world.

They called themselves **The Way**.

Their leader — a figure who would become known to history simply as **The Speaker** — addressed the United Nations in a language none of them spoke but all of them understood. Neural translation, we learned later. A gift from The Way's bio-sciences division.

Her message was simple. Four truths delivered without emotion:

1. **Earth is dying.** The core is destabilizing. The magnetosphere will fail within 200 years. The surface will become uninhabitable within 300. We already knew this, but we had buried the knowledge.

2. **We are the same.** Humanity has spread to the stars. The Way is one branch — the one that never forgot its origin world. They had been monitoring us for centuries, waiting until we were ready.

3. **We were never ready, but time has run out.** They had hoped to make contact later, after we had united as a species. But the window was closing. They acted.

4. **They offer a choice. Two paths. One planet.**

---

## Chapter 3: The Choice — Two Paths Divided

The Way laid out humanity's options in precise, contractual language. There was no malice in it — only the cold logic of a species that had done this before.

### Path One: Ascension — Joining The Way

Those who chose this path would be integrated into The Way's interstellar alliance. They would receive augmentation, education, and citizenship. They would leave Earth behind forever, their genetic legacy preserved among the stars.

The cost: They would never see their home world again. Earth would be mined to extinction. This was non-negotiable.

### Path Two: Stewardship — The Stratocolonies

Those who chose this path would ascend to stratospheric habitat stations — massive floating cities held aloft by anti-gravity cores licensed from The Way. They would oversee the planetary mining operations, extracting every resource of value from Earth's crust and mantle.

The cost: They became indentured to The Way. Bound by contract — the **Charter of Stewardship** — to deliver resource quotas in exchange for continued life support, technology licenses, and eventual access to the interstellar economy. They would watch their home world be hollowed out from above.

### The Third Path (Unofficial)

Some refused both options. These were the **Denied** — those who chose to remain on the surface and take their chances. The Way did not stop them. They simply noted that the surface would become uninhabitable within three generations and left them to their fate.

The Denied would become the ancestors of the surface tribes — the people the station-dwellers would later call the **Grudges**.

---

## Chapter 4: The Great Schism (2056–2062)

For seven years, humanity tore itself apart over the choice.

**The Exodus Faction** — those who favored joining The Way — argued that humanity's future was among the stars. To refuse the offer was suicide. They painted the Stratocolonists as cowards and the Denied as fools.

**The Stewardship Faction** — those who favored the colonies — argued that humanity must retain its connection to Earth. To leave was abandonment. To stay on the surface was death. The colonies were the only sane middle path.

**The Denied** — those who refused both — argued that The Way could not be trusted. That this was an extraction, not a rescue. That humanity should fight, not submit.

Wars were fought. Families were broken. By 2062, the Schism was complete.

- **12% of humanity** joined The Way and left for the stars.
- **68% of humanity** ascended to the Stratocolonies.
- **20% of humanity** remained on the surface as the Denied.

The Way began core-tapping operations in 2063. The surface became increasingly hostile. The Denied adapted, migrated, and survived — or died trying.

### The Charter of Stewardship

Every colonist is bound by the Charter. It is a contract, a constitution, and a cage.

- **Resource Quotas:** Each colony must deliver a minimum volume of refined materials to The Way every quarter. Failure results in reduced life support, tech sanctions, or — in extreme cases — dissolution of the colony.
- **Tech Licensing:** All advanced technology — anti-gravity, medical suites, energy weapons — is licensed from The Way. Reverse engineering is forbidden and punishable by exile.
- **The Debt:** Humanity owes The Way for their salvation. This debt is calculated in resource value and passed down through generations. No colonist is born free of it.
- **The Exile Clause:** Any colonist who violates the Charter may be stripped of station privileges and **Sentenced to Earth** — a punishment that is functionally a death sentence.

### Society in the Colonies

Life in the colonies is stratified. A rigid class system has emerged over centuries:

- **The Directorate:** The ruling council, one representative per colony. They hold ultimate authority under the Charter.
- **The Executives:** Corporate and administrative leaders who manage day-to-day operations.
- **The Technicians:** Engineers, scientists, medical professionals — the skilled labor that keeps the colonies running.
- **The Laborers:** The majority of colonists. They work the refineries, the farms, the recycling facilities.
- **The Unbound:** Criminals, dissidents, and debtors. Those who have lost their station privileges.

The colonies are not a utopia. They are a gilded cage — clean, orderly, and suffocating.

## Chapter 6: The Surface — The Grudges (2063–2400)

The Denied did not die.

They adapted, fractured, and evolved into something new. They became the **Grudges** — a name they wear with pride, a reminder of the debt owed to them by the Sky People and The Way.

### The Surface Today (2400)

The planet is scarred. The Way's core-tapping operations have rendered vast stretches uninhabitable — toxic atmospheres, seismic instability, volcanic activity that hasn't ceased in three centuries.

But pockets of life remain. The Grudges have learned to find them.

### The Surface Factions

**The Keepers of the Old Faith** — Religious movements that believe Earth is a living entity and The Way is a parasite. They perform rituals to "heal the land" and hoard pre-contact technology.

**The Tech-Scavengers** — Nomadic clans that hunt for old-world technology in the ruins. They are skilled mechanics, chemists, and hackers. They trade with the colonies through black-market channels.

**The Hollow Lords** — Warlords who control access to the deep shaft mines — the tunnel networks left behind by The Way's extraction operations. They rule through fear, controlling the most valuable resource on the surface: safe shelter.

**The Network** — A loose alliance of surface communities connected by a hardline data network running through the old fiber-optic lines that still function. They share information, resources, and warnings. They are the closest thing the surface has to a government.

**The Forgotten** — Isolated communities that have reverted to pre-industrial subsistence. They have no contact with the colonies and no interest in The Way. They have their own gods, their own wars, their own peace.

## Chapter 7: The Fallen — A Character Framework

The player character is a **Fallen** — someone who has been Sentenced to Earth, or has chosen to return, or has been displaced to the surface by circumstance.

### Reasons for Being Fallen

There is no single origin story. The Fallen are defined by their condition, not their history.

- **The Sentenced:** Convicted of violating the Charter. Crimes range from tech piracy to sedition to murder. The sentence is always the same: Earth.
- **The Crash:** Transport shuttles between colonies fail. Escape pods malfunction. Some Fallen arrive by accident, alive only because they were lucky.
- **The Volunteer:** Some choose this path. Researchers studying surface conditions. Explorers seeking lost technology. Pilgrims seeking truth.
- **The Exchanged:** A prisoner swap, a political trade, a family offered in exchange for debt relief. Some Fallen were never given a choice.
- **The Returned:** Born on the colonies but driven by a compulsion to see the home world with their own eyes. Some call them romantics. Most call them fools.
- **The Escaped:** They found a way off the colonies without authorization. Stole a shuttle. Bribed a pilot. Slipped through an airlock. Now they are wanted fugitives on both sides.

### The player character's family

Our character's family chose **Stewardship**. They ascended to the colonies when the Choice was made, watching their home world shrink below them. Generations lived and died in the sky, working the refineries, paying the debt, dreaming of ground they had never touched.

Something brought our Fallen down. Maybe it was a sentence. Maybe it was a choice. Maybe it was destiny.

The truth is out there — buried in the dirt, locked in the ruins, waiting in the deep places where The Way's machines still hum.

# Part II: Technologies

## Chapter 8: The Way's Technology — The Gift That Binds

The Way's technological base is thousands of years ahead of humanity's. They license only what is necessary for the mining operations — and only at the level required.

### Gravitic Technology

**Gravitic Repulsion Cores (GRCs)** — The heart of every Stratocolony. These devices generate a controlled anti-gravity field capable of lifting immense mass. Each colony has six primary cores and a dozen backups. The technology is a black box — colonists can maintain and replace them but cannot replicate or understand them.

**Personal Grav-Controllers** — Small devices that allow individuals to fly, slow falls, or carry massive loads. Licensed to colony security and emergency services only.

### Warp Technology

**Gate Network** — The Way has established warp gates at strategic points in the solar system. These allow instantaneous travel between gates. One gate is in Earth orbit, reserved for Way traffic. The colonies have no access to it.

**Ship Drives** — The Way's ships use compact warp drives. The technology is incomprehensible to human science. Attempts to reverse-engineer captured drives have all failed.

### Bio-Sciences

**The Panacea** — A universal treatment that The Way provides to colonists. It cures most diseases, halts aging, and grants immunity to known pathogens. It is administered monthly. Without it, colonists who descend to the surface face infection and rapid aging.

This is the ultimate control mechanism. The Way controls the distribution of Panacea. Exiles are denied it.

**Neural Translation** — Implants that allow real-time translation of any language. Standard issue for all colonists. Removed from exiles.

### Energy

**Zero-Point Extractors** — The Way's power source. They draw energy from vacuum fluctuations. Each colony has one primary extractor. They are the most heavily guarded and least understood devices in the colonies.

---

## Chapter 9: Colonial Technology — What Humans Built

Over 343 years, human ingenuity has flourished within the constraints imposed by The Way. Colonial technology is a mix of licensed Way tech, reverse-engineered discoveries, and purely human innovation.

### Power & Energy

**Fusion Micro-Reactors** — Human-designed fusion reactors that power the colonies' secondary systems. Safer and more efficient than pre-contact fusion. Used in shuttles, ground vehicles, and surface outposts.

**Thermal Harvesters** — Devices that convert geothermal heat into electricity. Used on the surface to power settlements near volcanic vents.

**Frictionless Turbines** — High-efficiency wind turbines that use magnetic levitation bearings. Common in surface settlements with consistent wind patterns.

### Medical & Biological

**Cellular Regeneration Tanks** — Medical pods that can heal severe trauma, regrow limbs, and cure most cancers. Available only in the colonies. A few have been smuggled to the surface.

**Genetic Recoding** — Techniques to modify human DNA. Used to treat inherited diseases, enhance physical capabilities, and adapt to low-oxygen or high-radiation environments. Illegal on most colonies without Directorate approval.

**Synth-Blood** — Artificial blood that carries oxygen more efficiently than natural blood. Gives recipients enhanced stamina and hypoxia resistance. Standard issue for colony security forces.

### Materials & Manufacturing

**Carbon-Weave** — A fabric stronger than steel, lighter than silk. Used for pressure suits, armor, and structural reinforcement. Human-designed.

**Self-Healing Polymers** — Materials that repair minor damage autonomously. Used in colony hulls, pressure vessels, and surface habitat walls.

**Molecular Forges** — 3D printers that work at the molecular level. Can produce almost any object from feedstock. Widespread in the colonies. Rare on the surface.

### Computing & Data

**Quantum Processors** — Computing cores that leverage quantum states for parallel processing. Colonial tech is primitive compared to The Way's, but still vastly superior to pre-contact computers.

**Personality Cores** — Experimental AI systems that can store and simulate a human consciousness. Controversial. Considered illegal on most colonies. Rumored to exist in secret research labs.

**Direct Neural Interfaces (DNIs)** — Implants that allow direct brain-computer interaction. Standard for technicians. Optional for others. Removed from exiles.

## Chapter 10: The Grudges' Technology — Scavenge, Repair, Improvise

The surface has no manufacturing base. Every piece of technology the Grudges possess has been salvaged, stolen, traded, or inherited. It is a patchwork civilization built on the bones of the old world.

### Weapons

**Rail Rifles** — Electromagnetic projectile weapons salvaged from pre-contact military stockpiles. Powerful but ammunition is rare. Only the Hollow Lords have them in numbers.

**Chemical Propellant Weapons** — Old Earth firearms. Simple, reliable, and ammunition can be manufactured. The most common weapon on the surface.

**Arc-Throwers** — Improvised energy weapons that use salvaged power cells. Unreliable but terrifying. A direct hit can incapacitate even an armored target.

**Blades** — The most common weapon. Made from salvaged metal, carbon-weave scraps, or carved bone. Every Grudge carries a blade.

### Tools

**Analyzers** — Handheld devices that can scan materials for composition, toxicity, and value. Salvaged from pre-contact scientific equipment. Priceless for scavengers.

**Atmosphere Processors** — Portable units that filter toxins from air. Essential for travel through dead zones. Powered by small fusion cells that last years.

**Signal Scanners** — Modified communication devices that can detect active electronics, power signatures, and data transmissions. Used to find hidden colony tech or avoid The Way's monitoring equipment.

### Vehicles

**Ground-Runners** — Modified pre-contact vehicles with electric or hydrogen fuel cell conversions. Reliable for short-range travel. Common in the Midwest territories.

**Hover-Rigs** — Salvaged anti-gravity lifters turned into personal transports. Rare. Most are owned by warlords or wealthy traders.

**Wind-Walkers** — Land-sail vehicles that use the constant winds of the plains. Silent, fast, and fuel-free.

### Medicine

**Herbal Medicine** — The Grudges have rediscovered and refined pre-contact botanical medicine. They can treat infections, set bones, and manage chronic conditions without Panacea.

**Radiation Purges** — A brutal detoxification process that removes radiation poisoning. Dangerous, painful, and not always successful. The only alternative to dying slowly.

**Stimpaks** — Chemically enhanced injectors that provide temporary boosts to strength, speed, or pain tolerance. Addictive. Manufactured in secret labs on the surface using scavenged precursors.

## Chapter 11: Powers & Anomalies

The Way's core-tapping operations have done something to Earth. The constant extraction of planetary mass and energy has created anomalies — places where the laws of physics seem to bend.

### Known Anomalies

**The Dead Zones** — Regions where the magnetosphere has collapsed entirely. Solar radiation bathes the surface. Electronics fail. Life cannot survive without heavy shielding. These zones are growing.

**The Gravity Wells** — Locations where gravity fluctuates unpredictably. In some places, you weigh half as much. In others, you're pinned to the ground. The Wells are dangerous but also valuable — some rare materials can only be harvested under high-G conditions.

**The Psychic Fields** — Rare and poorly understood. Humans exposed to certain energy signatures develop unusual abilities. Telepathy. Telekinesis. Precognition. The effects are unpredictable, often temporary, and sometimes fatal.

The Way denies these exist. The Directorate suppresses information about them. The Grudges whisper about them in the dark.

### Fringe Sciences

**Chronal Theory** — A controversial field of study that suggests The Way's core-tapping is affecting time itself in certain regions. Clocks run faster or slower in the anomaly zones. No one has proven it. No one has disproven it.

**Entropic Resistance** — Research into why some materials and organisms decay faster or slower in the anomaly zones. Has implications for everything from construction to medicine.

**Quantum Resonance** — The study of how human consciousness interacts with the quantum fields around the Wells. The basis for what the colonists dismiss as "superstition" and what the Grudges call "the Sight."

# Part III: The World

## Chapter 12: Geography of a Dying Planet

The surface is a patchwork of biomes, each shaped by the interaction of natural geography and The Way's extraction operations.

### The Dead Plains (Formerly the American Midwest)

Once the breadbasket of the world, now a toxic grassland. The soil is poisoned. The water is brackish. But life persists — hardy grasses, mutated animals, and scattered settlements of Grudges who have learned to filter the toxins from their food and water.

The stratocolonies are visible here, hanging in the sky like silver moons.

### The Spine (Formerly the Rocky Mountains)

High altitude, low radiation, abundant geothermal energy. The Spine is a haven for surface life. It's where the Network is strongest. It's where the oldest and most established surface settlements are found.

It's also where the Hollow Lords are strongest.

### The Sink (Formerly the Mississippi Basin)

A vast, stagnant marsh created by the collapse of the river system. The Sink is dangerous — toxic water, aggressive wildlife, and pockets of methane that can explode without warning.

It's also rich in salvage. The old river cities have sunk into the mire, their treasure waiting for those brave enough to dive.

### The Crustal Scar (The Way's Primary Excavation)

A wound in the planet's surface visible from orbit. The Scar is a deep canyon system — miles deep in places — where The Way's machines tear into the Earth's crust. The area is heavily guarded by automated drones and patrolled by Way security.

Approaching the Scar is suicide. But the Scar is also the source of the anomaly fields.

---

## Chapter 13: Bestiary — Life After the Fall

Life has adapted. Some of it has adapted in ways that are terrifying.

### The Scavengers

**Rust-Hounds** — Canine descendants, adapted to the toxic plains. They travel in packs. They are intelligent, cooperative, and hungry. They will eat anything.

**Crag-Stalkers** — Feline predators of the Spine. Solitary. Patient. They hunt by ambush, dropping on prey from above. Their claws can pierce carbon-weave.

**Sink-Crocs** — Massive reptiles that lurk in the Sink. They can remain motionless for days. They are ambush predators of terrifying patience and power.

### The Mutants

**The Shamblers** — Humans (or former humans) who were exposed to radiation and chemical waste without treatment. Their minds are gone. Their bodies are twisted. They are driven by a single impulse: hunger.

**The Glowing Ones** — Rare. Creatures — animal and human — that have absorbed so much radiation they emit their own light. They are dangerous to touch. They are also sought after by the Tech-Scavengers, who harvest their bioluminescent tissue for use in improvised light sources.

**The Deep Things** — Rumors from the Sink and the Scar. Creatures that live in the hot, dark places where The Way's machines have opened the crust. They are never seen clearly. They are only heard — and felt.

### The Remnants

**Watcher Drones** — The Way's automated surveillance units. They drift across the surface, monitoring activity. They do not attack unprovoked. But they report everything they see.

**Security Constructs** — Old-world military robots that still operate. Some are dormant. Some are active. Some have gone rogue and no longer recognize friend from foe.

**Titans** — The Way's mining platforms. Colossal machines miles across that crawl across the Scar, extracting and processing ore. They are slow, implacable, and utterly indifferent to human life.

## Chapter 14: Factions & Politics

### The Directorate (Stratocolonies)

The ruling body of the seventeen colonies. They are pragmatic, ruthless, and bound by the Charter. Their goal is survival — of the colonies, of humanity, of their own power.

- **Leader:** Director Marius Voss (Elysium)
- **Population:** ~45 million across all colonies
- **Goal:** Maintain the Charter, pay the debt, keep the lights on
- **Key Resource:** Licensed Way technology, Panacea distribution

### The Network (Surface)

The closest thing to a government on the surface. The Network connects settlements through the hardline data system and coordinates trade, defense, and information sharing.

- **Leader:** Council of Elders (rotating, one per settlement)
- **Population:** ~500,000 across the Spine
- **Goal:** Build a sustainable surface civilization independent of the colonies
- **Key Resource:** Knowledge, data, communication infrastructure

### The Hollow Lords (Surface — The Spine)

Feudal warlords who control access to the deep shaft mines. They rule through military force and control of essential resources.

- **Leader:** Lord Kael (the Iron Hand)
- **Population:** ~200,000 (subjects and slaves)
- **Goal:** Expand territory, control all surface mining, challenge the colonies
- **Key Resource:** Safe underground shelter, geothermal power, rail rifles

### The Keepers of the Old Faith (Surface — Scattered)

A decentralized religious movement that worships Earth as a living entity. They believe The Way is a cosmic parasite and that humanity's purpose is to heal the planet.

- **Leader:** The Voice (rotating oracle)
- **Population:** ~100,000 across small communes
- **Goal:** Heal the planet, drive away The Way, restore balance
- **Key Resource:** Ancient knowledge, botanical medicine, influence among the Grudges

### The Way (Extrasolar)

The interstellar human alliance that initiated contact. Their motives remain unclear. Some believe they are benevolent. Others believe they are conquerors. The truth is probably somewhere in between.

- **Leader:** The Speaker (unknown, possibly a rotating position)
- **Population:** Unknown, spread across hundreds of worlds
- **Goal:** Resource extraction, species preservation (humanity), unknown long-term objectives
- **Key Resource:** All advanced technology, warp travel, Panacea, the warp gate

### The Forgotten (Surface — Isolated)

Settlements that have no contact with the colonies, the Network, or anyone else. They have their own cultures, their own beliefs, their own ways of surviving.

- **Population:** Unknown, possibly hundreds of small communities
- **Goal:** Survival on their own terms
- **Key Resource:** Self-sufficiency, ignorance (the colonies don't know about them)

## Chapter 15: The Grudge — A Philosophy

To be a Grudge is to remember.

It is to remember that the Sky People left you behind. That The Way came with promises and took everything. That the planet you walk on is dying, and that someone, somewhere, owes a debt.

The Grudge is not just a name. It is a philosophy. A way of understanding the world.

**Grudge is Memory.** To hold a grudge is to remember what was done. The Grudges remember the Choice. They remember the betrayal. They remember every slight, every broken promise, every drop of blood.

**Grudge is Identity.** In a world that has tried to erase them, the Grudges define themselves by what they will not forget. Their grudges are their history, their culture, their reason for being.

**Grudge is Survival.** The anger, the resentment, the determination — these are fuel. They keep the Grudges going when hope fails. They are the fire that will not go out.

To be a Grudge is to carry the weight of the old world into the new one.

And one day — maybe — to settle the account.
